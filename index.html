THE LEGACY
TOP 10 ADIDAS IN BOSTON
The sneakers that built the culture. From the streets of Roxbury to the finish line on Boylston — these are the ten Adidas silhouettes that defined Boston.
No. 01
SUPERSTAR
1969 — SHELL TOE KING
Born on the basketball court, made immortal on the street. When Run-DMC released "My Adidas" in 1986, the Superstar became the official sneaker of hip-hop — and Boston's street scene never let it go. The shell toe was everywhere from Roxbury to Roslindale.
🏙️ BOSTON STREET ICON
No. 02
BOSTON SUPER
1984 — BORN FOR BOYLSTON
Created to honor the 88th Boston Marathon, the Boston Super is literally named for this city. With its mesh upper, suede overlays, and grippy outsole, it became a cult running classic. Decades later, Adidas resurrected its DNA in the NMD — proving Boston's influence never fades.
🏅 MADE FOR BOSTON
No. 03
CAMPUS
1970s — THE COLLEGE CLASSIC
Originally a basketball shoe, the Campus found its true home on college grounds and in hip-hop culture. Boston's universities kept this suede classic alive through every decade. From BU to Northeastern, the Campus was the go-to sneaker of the student who moved like they owned the block.
🎓 BOSTON CAMPUS STAPLE
No. 04
GAZELLE
1968 — SUEDE FOREVER
Introduced as a training shoe, the Gazelle became a fashion legend. Its slim suede silhouette made it a favorite across subcultures — from Brit mods to Boston b-boys. Light, clean, and effortlessly cool, the Gazelle proved you didn't need flash to make a statement.
🦌 BOSTON CLASSIC
No. 05
SAMBA
1950 — THE ORIGINAL
The oldest sneaker on this list and still the most relevant. Built for soccer players training on frozen fields, the Samba's gum sole and low profile made it a streetwear staple decades before that term existed. Boston's soccer community and casual scene kept the Samba running longer than any trend.
⚽ TIMELESS IN BOSTON
No. 06
STAN SMITH
1971 — CLEAN FOREVER
The minimalist's sneaker. Clean white leather, perforated three stripes — no logos screaming for attention. Boston has always appreciated that understated confidence. The Stan Smith was the choice of the prep school kid and the corner store regular alike. Still one of Adidas's best sellers to this day.
🤍 BOSTON CLEAN
No. 07
FORUM
1984 — HIGH TOP ROYALTY
The Forum was Adidas going all-in on basketball — a high-top with a velcro ankle strap built for the game but adopted by the street. In the '80s and '90s, Boston's B-boy and basketball communities wore the Forum like a badge. Its bold construction made it impossible to ignore.
🏀 B-BOY BOSTON
No. 08
NMD
2015 — THE BOSTON TRIBUTE
The NMD carries Boston in its DNA — literally. It was built by combining three archival Adidas models: the Rising Star, the Micropacer, and the Boston Super. Peak hype when it dropped, it showed that Boston's sneaker legacy was woven into the most modern silhouette Adidas had ever made.
🧬 BOSTON DNA INSIDE
No. 09
ULTRA BOOST
2015 — THE MARATHON SHOE
When Adidas launched the Ultra Boost, they called it the greatest running shoe ever made. Boston runners agreed — it became the shoe of choice along the Charles River and at the starting line in Hopkinton. Boost technology changed everything about how a shoe felt underfoot.
🏃 MARATHON READY
No. 10
TOP TEN
1979 — THE ORIGINAL TOP TEN
The original. Adidas developed the Top Ten by consulting actual NBA players — including Rick Barry — to build a shoe that handled every style of play. A full-leather high-top that commanded respect the moment you laced it up. Detroit loved it. Boston respected it. Street culture claimed it forever.
🔟 THE ORIGINAL
